Classification Definition Examples
American Society of Anesthesiologists I A normal healthy patient
  • Healthy
  • Non-smoking
  • No or minimal alcohol use
American Society of Anesthesiologists II A patient with mild systemic disease
  • Mild diseases without substantive functional limitations
  • Current smoker
  • Social alcohol drinker
  • Pregnancy
  • Obesity (BMI 30–39.9)
  • Well-controlled diabetes mellitus or hypertension
  • Mild lung disease
American Society of Anesthesiologists III A patient with severe systemic disease
  • Poorly controlled diabetes mellitus or hypertension
  • Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease
  • Morbid obesity (BMI ≥40)
  • Active hepatitis
  • Alcohol dependence or abuse
  • Implanted pacemaker
  • Moderate reduction of ejection fraction
  • End-stage renal disease on dialysis
  • History (>3 months) of myocardial infarction, stroke, or coronary artery disease with stents
American Society of Anesthesiologists IV A patient with severe systemic disease that is a constant threat to life
  • Recent (<3 months) myocardial infarction, stroke, or coronary artery disease with stents
  • Ongoing cardiac ischemia or severe valve dysfunction
  • Severe reduction of ejection fraction
  • Shock
  • Sepsis
  • Disseminated intravascular coagulation
  • Acute respiratory distress
  • End-stage renal disease not on dialysis
American Society of Anesthesiologists V A moribund patient who is not expected to survive without the operation
  • Ruptured abdominal or thoracic aneurysm
  • Massive trauma
  • Intracranial bleed with mass effect
  • Ischemic bowel with significant cardiac pathology or multiple organ dysfunction
American Society of Anesthesiologists VI A declared brain-dead patient whose organs are being removed for donor purposes
  • Brain-dead organ donor
